introduction

Start with a strong opening paragraph. Draw the reader in with a specific observation, a question, or a small story. Avoid preamble — get to the point quickly.

This is your second paragraph. Expand on the idea. You have room to breathe here — EB Garamond is a pleasure to read at length, so don't be afraid of longer paragraphs if the thought demands it.

A section heading

Use h2 headings to break up longer posts into scannable sections. Keep them short and direct — a heading is a promise to the reader about what comes next.

A blockquote for a passage you want to highlight, or for quoting someone else. Keep quotes purposeful — only pull in something that genuinely adds to the point.

Continue the thought after the blockquote. Quotes shouldn't end a section — always follow with your own words.

Another section

You can use lists when the content is genuinely list-shaped:

  • First item — keep list items roughly parallel in structure
  • Second item — avoid lists just to break up prose
  • Third item — if items need full sentences, prose is usually better

A smaller heading, if needed

Use h3 sparingly, for sub-sections within a section. Most posts won't need them. If you find yourself reaching for one often, the post might benefit from being split into two.

End with something that gives the reader a place to land. Not a summary — they just read it. A final thought, an open question, or a next step.
